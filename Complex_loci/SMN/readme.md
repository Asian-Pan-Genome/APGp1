# SMN locus decomposition

The **SMN** locus is one of the most complex regions in the human genome due to large-scale segmental duplications and high-identity gene conversions. We have decomposed this region into eight principal genomic blocks, defined as the fundamental *minimizer repeat units*. These units were identified through exhaustive pairwise sequence alignments across diverse SMN haplotypes.

The decomposition strategy presented here involves manual curation and is not a fully automated pipeline. For researchers looking to perform similar analyses on highly repetitive or duplicated loci, we recommend similar workflows like:

* PGGB: https://github.com/pangenome/pggb; see [Amylase paper](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07911-1))
* PGR-TK: https://github.com/GeneDx/pgr-tk
